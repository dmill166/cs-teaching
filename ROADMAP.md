# Dakota Learns — Roadmap

> Build-in-public. The "why" behind decisions lives in `life-os` (private); execution lives on the GitHub Project board (Phase = milestone).

---

## Vision

**Dakota Learns is a free, beginner-first home for learning — starting with computer science — taught clearly and honestly by someone still learning too.** The end state is a *lasting, trustworthy open-education brand* that:

1. makes CS genuinely learnable from zero (no prerequisites, no gatekeeping),
2. doubles as Dakota's tested-experience teaching portfolio, and
3. becomes self-sustaining (sponsors / optional paid extras) **without ever gating the free core.**

The free core is the constant; everything else is built around it.

## Principles (non-negotiables)

- **Free core, always.** No paywall on learning. Support is optional; you never hit a wall.
- **Dakota authors his own voice.** AI scaffolds, drafts in his voice for review, and edits — he owns the final word.
- **Evidence-based pedagogy.** Retrieval + spaced practice, dual coding, PRIMM, worked examples.
- **Portable, not parochial.** Courses are framed by subject; university alignment is a single footnote.
- **Build in public.** Honest about what's done and what isn't.
- **Privacy-respecting.** No ad-tech, no tracking-for-sale.

## Milestones (board `Phase`)

> Each has a clear "done looks like." Near-term milestones are dated on the board's Roadmap view; far ones get dated as they approach. Backlog refinement happens at the start of each milestone (epics → action items + definitions of done).

### M0 — Foundations laid · **shipped**
Platform exists, rules are set: site live; build-time content sync; auto-generated nav; cross-repo auto-build; subject-first repo naming; per-topic teaching framework; authorship/voice principle; Zelle 4e reconciliation; Discussions; public-surface polish (disclaimers, abstraction, RSS).

### M1 — CS1 teachable spine (weeks 1–5) · **current**
*Done looks like:* the first five weeks exist as real materials, and the framework is **validated by building Week 1 end-to-end** (then refined before templating).

### M2 — CS1 complete & teach-ready · **target: Fall 2026**
*Done looks like:* all 15 weeks built, assessments + autograding in place, good enough to (a) submit as a tested-experience portfolio and (b) teach from. Institution-specific boilerplate is added at teach-time.

### M3 — Content engine humming
*Done looks like:* a sustainable build-in-public cadence with distribution — the "Teaching CS1 in public" series, RSS, LinkedIn syndication, private analytics.

### M4 — Framework proven → second course
*Done looks like:* the framework has produced one complete course; a second (CS Principles or CS2) is scoped + started, textbook chosen at scope time.

### M5 — Sustainability
*Done looks like:* the project can fund its own time without gating the free core — matured sponsors, optional paid *extras*, comments once readership justifies it.

### M6 — Multi-course catalog + beyond CS
*Done looks like:* the planned courses exist; the subject-agnostic brand is exercised with a first non-CS exploration.
